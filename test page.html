While it's no secret that Sturgeon's Law is in full effect with game mods, some of them stand out due to their sheer awfulness. You have to wonder how much these developers like these games if they managed to screw up so badly.

    Doom 
With over 15,000 Doom mods (and many more to come) on the /idgames archives, there are many horrible ones to go around as the ones below demonstrate.

  • In Doom's heyday, many shovelware discs were created by low-budget companies to cash in on its popularity. They were invariably huge collections of WADs downloaded online and put on a disc without considerations of quality or permission from their designers. Their only redeeming quality was that they were helpful to some players who didn't have the resources to check out WADs online. A few examples:
    • WizardWorks published D!ZONE, a series of CDs sold containing hundreds of WADs collected from both the BBSes of the time and the early Internet. A large chunk of them are horrible, most of them unfinished, and some won't even load correctly at all. The back of the boxes often show stuff that isn't even in the game. Several YouTube users started a series called The D!Zone Experience to showcase some of the weirder WADs included.
    • Determined to beat these distributors at their own game, id Software included a disc known as Maximum Doom as bonus content to the Master Levels for Doom II. The Master Levels Disc, often accompanied by a book, contains 1,830 amateur WADs mostly for Doom II—with a great number of them being Doom WADs turned into Doom II WADs using a converter that includes Doom II monsters by randomly replacing exactly one monster each per level. A community member named Tarnsman started a series of livestreams on his Twitch account called "The Great Shovelware Extravaganza" in which he plays through Maximum Doom's Doom II content, on which he spent over 60 hours. His experience was full of shoddy design, beautifully bad texture use, and bugs, including an example that defies explanation.note As this video reveals, this was another Doom WAD converted to one for Doom II, further highlighting the primitive quality of the converter they used
  • The 1996 Doom WAD "Boom v2.3" (not to be confused with the Boom source port) gained infamy with a review on the classic Doom Underground website declaring it to be the worst such addon the writer had ever seen. It features horrible custom MS Paint graphics for the wall textures (slathered with smiley faces) and the HUD (a solid-yellow rectangle), and sound effects that are unfitting at best (breaking glass for the shotgun?) and annoying at worst (the author shouting things with sound filters applied). The first episode's levels are poorly balanced (you get a BFG with full ammo on the first level with a tiny number of enemies), badly designed (you can often access the exit immediately), and little more than freehand scribbles—and yet one level manages to repeat itself, only with an edit that makes it easier to complete. The other two episodes are a slew of repeated levels that the author admits are terrible and yet still felt the need to include. You can see it for yourself here. The text file claims that it's a "MAJOR upgrade" from the now-missing v2.2, which just makes one wonder how bad the previous version was.
  • The works of Jerry Lehr, Jr. seldom rise above the level of the community's earliest custom maps, even the most mediocre, because they mainly consist of rooms and corridors with little to no height variation and lousy gameplay consisting of shooting clumps of low-level monsters with a shotgun or chaingun.
    • The first of his Nightmares trilogy which was the second ever WAD rejected from the /idgames archive for quality reasons—and his early WAD pack, which is worse than most people's early WADs because its four WADs largely consist of the same flat and simplistic level repeated over and over again with different object and hazard placement each time.
    • His last known release, Nightmares of Loki 1999, takes the cake. It combines the problems of flatness and bad gameplay with annoying sound effects, custom textures made up mostly of Doom textures repainted in eye-searing colors, and seemingly endless switch hunts that defy human logic, to the point that cheat codes may be required to solve certain levels. Not that you have that luxury at first, because the cheat codes have also been changed for some reason. Unsurprisingly, Jerry Lehr, Jr. later admitted that he made the WAD while addicted to marijuana. A review of Nightmares of Loki 1999 on Doomworld following its 2003 rerelease can be found here.
  • Doom: Rampage Edition was eagerly anticipated, featuring the premise of a Baron of Hell who escapes a UAC lab, allowing the players to play Doom from the monsters' side. Unfortunately, much to the disappointment of many, after three years of work, it failed to live up to its promises. The WAD features 60 megabytes of stolen music that do little else but bloat the WAD to 80 megabytes (that's three quarters of the archive), straining net connections of its time. Users who got past that were treated to sloppy graphics—the Baron's hand is just a recolored fist with badly drawn green flames and the skyboxes are just wall textures with regular sky above them—boring and repetitive gameplay, and the illogical ability of the baron to use guns. These include a Cacodemon gun launching a fireball that punches through enemies and kills most of them in one hit, and a shotgun and dual chainguins with infinite ammo you can collect very early on. You can use the Baron's usual abilities, the fireball and the claw, except they both use the aforementioned edited fist and the fireball has no proper firing frame. All of this and more won Rampage Edition Worst Wad at the very first Cacowards in 2004. The author's hostile reaction to its cold reception didn't help matters. It's so bad that one community member, Deathbringer, said he could "pull a better wad out of [his] ass." In a little over three weeks, he made a wad titled such: "A better wad i pulled out of my ass". Rampage Edition would later be made obsolete when a far superior mod, Doom: Empathy, later released, reintroducing the play-as-demons concept with far better execution.
  • Nazi Auferstehung: A DukenDoom Adventure won Worst Wad at the 2006 Cacowards for a variety of reasons, including sloppy textures, poorly credited use of assets from other users' mods, a use of outdated community Freedoom assets and an automatic level generator named SLIGE with barely noticeable edits thrown in, as well as an ungodly annoying and repetitive line of Duke Nukem saying "Die, you son of a bitch" every time you gib an enemy (or yourself if you input a suicide code in a source port that has one). Its initial beta was filesize bloated by a truckload of MP3s that don't play as music in-game just like Rampage Edition. What makes it worse is that the MP3s are said to have been consisted of MIDI recordings, making it further redundant on top of multiple copies of Doom II assets, which borders on downright illegal. Also following in the footsteps of Rampage Edition, its author reacted angrily to criticism, which led to a flame-war resulting in discussion of Dark Exodus's attempt at faking autism as well as a slew of unrelated topics. He was eventually banned for trolling and seemingly disappeared from the Internet entirely. Years later, newer, cleaner, smaller versions of the mod were released, this time with MIDI files for music.
  • Giulio "Glassyman" Galassi created Gamarra's Story, a series of maps that were widely reviled for their weak design and dull gameplay. This led him to lash out with Gamarra's Soul Story, a series of unfunny "joke wads" that, on top of sharing all the flaws of his previous work, was created solely to enact a childish revenge fantasy against his critics by hiring the player to kill them for insulting his work. It got bad enough that his Worst Wad Cacoward in 2007 came with a plea by the reviewer to stop making WADs altogether. Glassyman was eventually banned from almost the entire Doom community, and little has been heard of him since.
  • The user mattbratt11 achieved notoriety the same year with Doomworld posts consisting mainly of bizarre ideas for Doom mods and reacting badly to criticism of those ideas. His one released WAD is Mattbratt's Level Pack, a full megawad where the first four maps are stolen from other authorsorigins MAP01 is "Symphony of Death" by Sphagne, MAP02 is "A Monster Too Many" by XXoXX, MAP03 is "Cyber Fun" by Dimiter Georgiev, and MAP04 is "SQUARES" by Costa Lappas and the rest consist of rooms and corridors with little to no height variation that are mostly textured in the same monotonous shades of gray. He also cared so little about balance that he encouraged the use of cheats to beat it, including an infinite ammo patch stolen from the DeHackEd distribution. A video review of the WAD can be found here.
  • The works of a troll known only as "Terry":
    • Terry had a catalog of levels designed to lure the player in with decent visuals, and then catch them in a "trap" subjecting them to all sorts of Sensory Abuse, including extremely loud noises, rapidly flashing lights and vulgar text popping up all over the screen. As if that wasn't bad enough, Terry had a group of fanboys (assuming they weren't just aliases) who imitated his shock humor; "Terrywad" eventually became a pejorative term on the /idgames archives. Even worse, several of Terry's wads were actively malicious: some actively tampered with your Zandronum configuration; others were actual zip bombs that disguised themselves as small archive files, only to unzip into petabytesnote one petabyte equals one thousand terabytes of nested folders, causing affected computers to run out of memory.
    • Terry's most infamous creation was the Zandronum WAD UAC Military Nightmare. On top of its idiotic gameplay and incredibly loud sound effects, the scripting featured a nonsensical plot involving John Romero heads, poorly-drawn MS Paint faces, a Bad Santa, and a truckload of vulgar references to anal rape. One level is actively malicious, in that it tampers with your source port configuration and renames your character to an anti-gay slur. UAC Military Nightmare won Worst Wad at the 2008 Cacowards, accompanied by a vitriolic guest rant almost as vulgar as the WAD itself, concluding with the guest deciding to give the award to his own anus instead of the WAD.
    • On May 4, 2014, UAC Military Nightmare and several of its successors and imitators were purged from the /idgames archives, as part of a crackdown on misleading and malicious WADs — including zip bombs and WADs that tamper with source port configurations. Two days later, a separate directory was created to quarantine the rest of the "Terrywads" away from the regular archive. No tears were shed in the community — except, perhaps, by the WADs' creators and their fans. As of The New '20s, the heat around the Terry wads has died down, along with Terry wads themselves. A YouTuber named Aquarius199 has made over 350 videos of Doom wads gameplay, and about 75% of these document Terry WADs.
  • The works of Doomguy 2000:
    • Doomguy's Warzone, not to be confused with something with the same name that came out years earlier. It's a gameplay mod with far too many unnecessary difficulty modes, badly overpowered custom weapons, and too many ungodly annoying or lethally aggravating custom enemies amongst the randomly generated roster, and almost all of the resources are plagiarized. There are also a ton of custom items that either break the game or are utterly useless. On top of all this, the author loved to advertise this WAD, even when told by seemingly the whole Doom community to stop, earning him a reputation as an Attention Whore.
    • His other releases aren't much better. They're largely intentionally provocative concept WADs that could be considered "avant-garde" if one were charitable, from 30,000 Levels (30,000 of the same box with an exit switch repeated over and over), to Blind Doom and Seizure Doom (exactly what those imply, with the bonus that Blind Doom still has visible floor and ceiling textures), to The Worst Level Ever Made (also precisely that, a player is put in a simple box with height, width and length having the amount of 1 each and nothing else). These WADs and the aforementioned Terrywads ultimately led to the Worst Wad Cacoward being discontinued in 2011, as it was originally meant for unintentionally bad work and awarding it to WADs made bad on purpose attracted trolls and caused it to lose most of its meaning.

    Fire Emblem 
Fire Emblem has an extensive hacking community, so a lot of stinkers turn up. One of the biggest problems is that most "total conversion" hacks tend to get abandoned before the halfway point; it's rare for most of these hacks to ever make it past the first few chapters before ending abruptly and leaving whatever story was being established up in the air. Meanwhile, "balance" or "challenge" hacks are either laughably unbalanced or unfairly difficult, and may also be buggy due to a lack of testing. Some other extremely common issues for any type of hack are poorly designed maps with equally poor enemy placement, high level foes that the player must defeat long before it would be feasible for them to do so, unbalanced character stats and growths that either make the game too easy (no one has any poor stats at any point, resulting in an army of juggernauts) or too hard (none of the characters' stats go up reliably, resulting in a weak team well into high levels), terrible sprite work, and poor quality music.

  • Tales of the Emblem is the epitome of lazy hacking. It simply replaces every major character in The Sacred Stones (Lyon being the odd exception) with Tales characters. It also horribly breaks every character, gives stat boosters more than one use and gives almost everyone terrible, terrible sprites with badly inserted blinking frames.
  • There's also Fire Emblem: Different Dimensions: Ostian Princess, or FEDDOP, which is what you will be after playing it. The design decisions make hardly any sense, the plot (which stars Lilina's right-out-of-nowhere daughter Lilian) has so many holes that it might as well not exist, there's hardly any difficulty at all and after clearing chapter 13 the game goes right back to chapter 12, causing an infinite loop. It's broken on a completely hilarious level. If you like, you may experience a Let's Play here.
    • Much of the game is simply Fire Emblem: The Blazing Blade with a sloppy find-and-replace job done to its script. One one hand, much of the new dialogue is dedicated to trying to shoehorn the events of the original game into its new context, handwaving everything that doesn't make sense with bad jokes and fourth-wall breaks. For stuff that isn't changed, it is now either spoken by different characters that now act massively out-of-character (Legault and Oswin are now generic bandit bosses), or by new characters regardless of whether it now makes sense (Sain is replaced with a 10-year old boy named Axel, but much of Sain's flirtatious behaviour towards women receive no edits).
    • The actual gameplay is hilariously imbalanced. There are barely any promoted enemies by the time you've recruited several, every playable character comes with a ridiculous and nonsensical inventory by default, and most unit statlines are extremely inconsistent and often broken (Zach, a unit who's theoretically meant to be a Fragile Speedster thanks to his high Speed stat, somehow has no Constitution (not zero Constitution, no number whatsoever) meaning that he takes a massive penalty to his Speed stat with most weapons and needs to rely on his Strength to carry him). Natalie, a one-off unit from the original game that you had to defend in a Protection Mission, replaces Merlinus as your Transporter. But because her 'game over on death' flag isn't changed, you are given a unit with terrible stats that cannot fight back, and will make you lose if she does die.
  • Corrupt Theocracy is another disastrous one. It claims to be a "semi-custom" hack, which means that the sprites are either splices/recolors of pre-existing sprites or are just the original sprites with no modification at all. It also means that many of the maps are just maps from FE7 itself, just with changed enemy placement and a ramped up difficulty. The maps that are new are universally poor, and often the only "challenge" in any of the maps is dealing with hordes of pathetically weak enemies, usually mixed in with a couple of obscenely powerful ones. The characters are horribly unbalanced, with many of them being so overpowered that the weapon triangle that defines Fire Emblem gameplay is completely irrelevant here. The story, while not as bad as Ostian Princess, is poorly written, rife with grammatical errors, and mostly told through tedious narration rather actually showing the player anything, and includes several incredibly clumsy attempts to tackle "adult" subject matter but end up just being uncomfortable to read. There are also a number of bugs, such as being unable to look at the stats of the lord of the first part of the game, certain weapons not displaying their stats properly, the screen going black at the start of certain chapters, and the inexplicable return of every single character from the first part of the game once the player gains access to the prep screen in the second part. Fortunately, the creator learned from their mistakes and would later go on to create both The Sun God's Challenge and Fire Emblem: The Sun God's Wrath, the latter of which in particular is very well-regarded.
  • Duo Geno Escapo is often considered one of the worst Fire Emblem hacks of the FE Builder era. The graphics and sprites that were changed from the base game are either unconvincing splices of already existing sprites with clashing palettes added in, or recycled portraits from other FE games. The gameplay is horribly unbalanced (enemies tend to be tediously, overwhelmingly strong, rigging is needed to get past some sections, there are a lot of gameplay-related glitches and map design is generally uninspired and awful, with some maps being based off Pokémon routes and using tilesets wrong), and the music ranges from merely okay to overly loud and earsplitting, with a rather good amount of tracks also being very quiet in contrast. But what pushes this hack over the edge is the writing, with humor that is just plain unfunny even when it doesn't contain tasteless, offensive jokes that only cross the line once. A nonsensical, hard-to-follow story, protagonists that are the very definition of Designated Heroes, and horrible, unintentionally funny quotes singlehandedly made a rather big part of the community turn against not just Duo Geno, but also against this kind of lower-effort humor-based hacks. While the creator is too willing to promote it to be ashamed of it, he agrees with all the criticism the hack has gotten, claiming its overall unpolished nature to be due to him making the hack with a friend over the course of two years while the two were still immature and new to hacking, and only thinking of releasing it when it was halfway completed. Still, you would think they would try to touch it up a bit before its release. The hack was removed from FE Universe in early 2020 due to a controversy that arose when a similarly offensive hack had its thread closed, but you can read an archive of the thread here, and a patch of the hack itself is still available in Romhacking.net.
  • Then there's the infamous Gheb FE and Gheb Saga, starring Gheb of FE8 as the protagonist. While they were technically competent compared to other hacks at the time they were made, they're both marred by unbalanced gameplay that seems designed less to challenge the player and more to simply aggravate them. Worse still, the "story" of both is less an actual narrative and more an excuse for extremely vulgar "humor", most frequently involving rape and pedophilia, but without any actual jokes, simply having characters say and do terrible things with very few attempts at actual comedy to make it even a little more palatable. Due to all this, both games (especially the first) developed an especially unpleasant reputation, and their own creator regrets making them.

    MUGEN 
M.U.G.E.N is a freeware fighting game engine that lets players design their own fighters. Some are good, or even great. Others... aren't. Some of the particularly bad ones are referred to as "Retarded Characters" in the community due to said characters having horrible controls, badly coded features, and deplorable spritework. In some cases, the spritework may be really good, but the characters are blatant ripoffs of existing characters, which are called "spriteswaps". These characters tend to have pretty awful controls and features. It doesn't help that many of them have jacked-up stats that make them overpowered. Given the fact that the spriteswaps' code is meant for a completely different character, hilarity is bound to ensue even if there aren't any modifications. Some creators have even done spriteswaps of their own characters.

  • Kong's characters are known to be extremely glitchy, overpowered to the point of being broken, or both. Mentioning Kong is calling a flame war upon yourself, and for good reason. Most infamously, one of his characters (Omega Red) was able to beat Rare Akuma on hard AI, when the latter of which is a character purposefully designed to be overpowered and undefeatable by a skilled MUGEN character maker.
  • "Raruto Full Game", a game based on a Naruto parody whose whole roster comprises of poorly coded Kung Fu Man edits. Even worse, all of the stages available in the "game" are stolen from other sources. Here's a peek at said horrid characters getting beaten up, which is far more enjoyable than the "full game" itself.
  • Warner's Vampire Burns is a spriteswap of the already-horrible Jedah by Kong. Not only doe it fix none of the glitches (including infinite air dashing - in a character with homing air-only projectiles), but isn't even that great of a spriteswapping job due to the obvious paint-over on most of its sprites. Warner was already getting a bad reputation for blatantly stealing other people's characters to make his spriteswaps, but this effectively tanked his credibility among the M.U.G.E.N. community.
  • Actarus's infamous, badly glitched Peter Griffin. It's very bloated in its stats, incredibly overpowered, and nigh-invulnerable due to a severe shortage of hitboxes, alongside stealing and messing with the sprites of an intro of Warner's earlier version of Homer Simpson where Peter is featured as a cameo and having the worst sounds for a M.U.G.E.N. character. See it in action here. The same creator has many more characters with similar problems, all of which have been beaten down by various people.
  • Almost all of KoopaKingdom.com's characters are spriteswaps of Mortal Kombat characters, poorly made into Nintendo characters. Spriteswapping is far from the only flaw present in KoopaKingdom.com's characters, as they also possess ridiculously powerful attacks, high priority on all of their attacks, spriting issues that aren't related to the spriteswapping, glitches, and several characters still having their pre-spriteswap sounds (such as Yoshi sounding like Scorpion). Don't just take our word, see them in all of their unglory here.
  • One particularly awful character creator is GooGoo64. He combines the horrible-to-the-point-of-game-breaking coding of Kong or Ainotenshi with the spriting styles of some of RyouWin's earlier Marvel vs. Capcom 2 characters (which amounts to using a capture card to get footage and then manipulating it into sprites for the character). His characters have unblockable moves, moves that render the character invincible while using them, one-hit kills, and various other problems. But one that stands out even among this crowd of miserable failure is his version of Gold Lightan from Tatsunoko vs. Capcom, which turned out to be a spriteswap of an MvC-style Ryu but with massively jacked-up stats; to make it worse, while the character is open-source, he didn't bother to give credit to the original creator or even changed the file names. The_None, creator of several much better Joke Characters, made an extended video beatdown series where he showcases each of GooGoo64's aberrations' broken moves, explaining additional bugs and subsequently beating them legitly (if possible at all). You can start from here.
  • Another infamous creator (though less well-known than Actarus or GooGoo64) is Pgrs111Magen. To start, every single character of his is a spriteswap, encompassing a wide variety of characters. While most of his characters are badly done Touhou characters, a few are hate characters of people he doesn't like, most likely because they bashed his works.   • Mortal Kombat Chaoticnote as well as its older incarnation, Mortal Kombat Anthology, and its original incarnation Mortal Kombat Special Edition is a grandiose example of quantity over quality: despite the promising main menu screen and its incorporation of even the most obscure MK charactersnote Duroc, Belokk, Karbrac and Hydro to name a few, the game thrives off of stolen characters and stages from all across MUGEN databases, as evidenced by the wildly varying quality of them. It has a particularly large roster of 300+ characters, and an additionally gigantic list of stages. However, despite this (or perhaps because of this), the game is extremely buggy and is prone to having major glitches: the music stutters, glitches or comes to a total stop, characters freeze in place even after the match, which can lead to the game softlocking. On top of the softlocks, the game is also prone to freezing, or just outright crashing for no reason whatsoever. While it is unknown if the game devs made any of the characters, the roster consists of multiple variations of several characters (including over 17 Sub-Zeroes), yet the majority of them play exactly the same way, even if some are completely nonsensical note Plasma Kang and Red Scorpion are prime examples. Other characters are also bizarre and poorly made, with either junky or downright abysmal sprites note  Bear Sub-Zero (yes, really), Daegon, Shadow Priest(s), Blaze, etc. - horribly broken, beyond cheap AI even for MK standards note Sensei Liu Kang, Fronzen (sic) Sub-Zero, etc. - utterly ridiculous Game Breakers that can end a match in seconds note  Riuky, Simon, Hsu Hao (again, we're serious), etc. - and nonsensical Original Generation characters that are either given no backstory note  the rather infamous Pantherk, Fusion, Nightspirits and a black-garbed ninja simply named Generick Ninja, or otherwise simply shoehorned into the game's intro storytelling.note Unknown "dark forces and Satanic" which "secretly joined" the MK tournament during the events of 3, Akuma being in the game due to "mixed dimensions". The intro itself is just a butchered, Engrish edition of UMK3's intro with some other spontaneous subplots shoehorned in. The game also has poor character announcements, some with different voice clips mashed together that do not match each other, poorly made original voice clips (such as for Sensei Liu Kang and the Fro(n)zen Sub-Zeroes), and some using text-to-speech. The fatalities, when they're not just canonical ones, are also either nonsensical, painfully mundane (several fatalities are just different methods of decapitation), or they simply do not work (Daegon's fatality has him do an uppercut...that misses, and then a second later the opponent drops dead). MRGSTAR321 shows the "chaotic" disaster in its purest in his second playthrough of the game, while Joel of Vinesauce fame has his fun trashing the project as well.
  • WlanmaniaX's Darkwing Duck is infamous for his notoriously awful animations, with all his kicks repeating Darkwing's walking animation, useless attacks due to small hitboxes and uninspired and hard-to-perform specials and hypers - even if performed correctly, several attacks are poorly coded to the point of causing debug errors. While Wlan's previous characters (Zim and Stimpy) were updated in response to criticism, Darkwing's reception was so bad that he was abandoned altogether by his own author in favor of Aperson98's better-received version.

    Sonic the Hedgehog games 
  • One Sonic Mania modder by the name of SonicOnBox is infamous for pumping out extremely low-quality mods at a near-constant rate. Most levels have minimal changes aside from the sprites (which are usually just poorly-imported stills of 3D models), and the few mods that do have new level design are incredibly blocky or linear and don't integrate with the environment at all. While this is bad enough, what truly puts him here is the fact that he occasionally steals assets from other mods without crediting the original modder or asking them for permission to use said assets. He also heavily moderates the comments on his channel to censor any negative comments about his work.
    • He's also known for stealing and repackaging mods, such as GPF's Movie Sonic mod for Sonic the Hedgehog (2006). He's been caught for this though.
  • A user named SuperSonicThomas has several mods that fit this.
    • One of his mods, Sonic the Hedgehog 5, is infamous for having assets stolen from other modders. This includes level design, sound, music, etc.
    • Another one of his mods, the now deleted Flaming Hills was also known for also having stolen assets, as well as broken level design, unfair object placement, etc.
  • Sea Gate in Sonic Forces was one of RobowilOFFICIAL's earliest mods, and let's just say that it was pretty terrible. The camera was broken, the object placement, while faithful, was completely bare-bones, and that it was simply painful to play. The creator actually hates it, to the extent where he flat-out deleted it. Thankfully, he learned from this, as while his Generations Crisis City in Sonic Forces mod was considered So Okay, It's Average, it was notably much more playable.
  • One noticeably strange mod for Sonic 1 is "Eggman the Dictator in Sonic the Hedgehog" which stars Doctor Robotnik himself as the main character. At first glance, one might think the mod would be an interesting take on Villain Protagonist; it is instead an unfortunate mess of poorly-made sprite frames, broken level textures, and strange modified game physics, to name a few things. Created by otherwise experienced Sonic modder "LOst", it gained notoriety after several YouTubers came across the mod.

  • FNaF: Left To Rust is a disaster. It uses various assets from the official games, and even a few from other fangames. Its story is positively nonsensical, perhaps even more than The Return to Freddy's; the "introduction" reads like a 10-year-old's poorly written Fan Fic. The animations are hastily made and incredibly janky, in both gameplay and cutscenes. The voice acting is pathetic and lifeless, and the dialogue is an edgy, ad-libbed mess. The audio is outrageously, unbearably loud at all times, even in the main menu (which uses a reversed "Toreador March"). The visuals are positively ugly; the textures on the obvious SFM models are a mess, the pizzeria itself looks dingy and wretched, and everything is festooned with vibrant greens, yellows, and reds. The gameplay is all over the place; it's glitchy, it has poorly executed Unexpected Gameplay Changes, and there are far too many animatronics and mechanics. If you manage to beat the game, you get a very poor cutscene in which Springtrap kills William with a gun, while incessantly swearing and spouting nonsense; it's got a Sequel Hook, too, but the game was never updated again. IULITM showcases the game without commentary in several of his videos.

    Other 
  • The Megaman isn't even made in Game Maker, but rather Game Maker Lite. That should tell you what you're in for. It's extremely linear. The only weapon you get is the Mega Buster, whose ammo count goes into the negatives. The physics are completely off; pivot points are poorly placed on Mega Man's sprite, causing him to teleport a little when he turns around. The signature sliding move is utterly broken; it just warps Mega Man forward for a second and then returns him to his original position. Most stages are ripped off from official Mega Man games (the rest are ripped off from other NES games), but all of them are broken into single screens with no transitions at all. The enemies and bosses fight nothing like their established counterparts; most enemies will overwhelm you with a Zerg Rush or Bullet Hell. The first two bosses' sound effects are horribly compressed. The user interface is the laziest; text is presented with the default Game Maker dialogue boxes (and riddled with typos), and the health bar, for whatever reason, is displayed on the game window's title bar, next to the "Minimize" and "Close" buttons. (But ammo and boss health are in the game window itself.) And, as if to add insult to injury, pressing Enter at any point instantly starts the ending "cutscene".
  • Metroid; Beginings (sic) is a very Obvious Beta. Samus frequently glitches out and falls through the floor, causing the game to crash. The game has no save feature; if you die, you have to replay the entire game. It doesn't feel like a Metroid game at all, with no exploration or non-linearity.note Bizarrely, the creator said this was deliberate because he "didn't want to plagiarize". So why bother making it a fan-game at all? The plot is almost nonexistent, and what little is there is all over the place. The art style is spectacularly ugly. The bosses are all insanely hard in the worst possible way.note Biodronider is entirely luck-based; the Space Pirate boss is fought underwater and almost impossible to avoid thanks to the floaty controls; and Dark Samus has a shockwave attack that's impossible to dodge, and also has a tendency to fall out of the level. And there's literally No Ending — the guy playing it hacked it with a Flash decompiler but found nothing programmed after the final boss fight.
  • While Plants vs. Zombies is a well-loved game, this NES bootleg version of it is horrendous. The first problem with it is the music, which is bad even for bootleg standards. Once you actually get into the game, you'll notice that the sprites are very Off-Model (Peashooter and Repeater outright don't have any color, simply blending into the color of the tile they're placed on) and none of the plants move at all. The game is also extremely difficult to the point of cheapness: a zombie appears before you can even place a Sunflower down, and the Potato Mine, which is meant to help you start in a round, is just as expensive as a Peashooter, making it entirely useless. But the worst part is that it seems that only half the sprites load most of the time, as the original game wasn't made for consoles with sprite limits. This all adds up to destroy the enjoyability the original had and isn't even fun if you're looking for Bile Fascination.
  • Quest for Magma Mountain is a shmup-type Pokémon game where you play as a Heatmor trying to go up Magma Mountain. The game unravels into a molten mess of an Obvious Beta as it goes on. Its biggest problem is its sloppy implementation of Dynamic Difficulty; the enemies' health increases exponentially as you progress, the protagonist is painfully slow, and letting an enemy pass costs you a life. All this combines to make a game where the enemies have more health than you have firepower; the "shiny" enemies with regenerating HP are functionally immortal, guaranteeing loss of a life. And buying upgrades also increases the enemies' health, which defeats the purpose of upgrading in the first place (not that the single-use items are useful to begin with). Buttons and icons vanish periodically. The sprites sometimes fail to animate. The score ranking system is broken and always overwrites your prior rank, regardless of if it was better or worse. You can get stuck on the results screen and have to reset. But the kicker is the horribly crass story, in which the protagonist comes off as a mass murderer trying to restore a group of villainous oppressors to power. Even if you could theoretically reach the Final Boss, the game has No Ending; it just boots you back to the map afterwards.
  • One-WAY is a Yume Nikki fan game in which you play as Aitsuki and explore your dream world. It's undone by its incredibly unfitting music (which may have been stolen), MS Paint backgrounds, and horrendous sprites (if they weren't stolen or recolored). It even stole some sprites from Ib and used them for a totally unrelated effect.
  • Slender Graveyard is not so much a fangame as a threepenny rip-off of Slender with worse graphics, pointless stolen stock assets, horrible map design, and inexcusable sound design, including audio levels that must have been taken in the dark. Any attempt at a scare is either apropos of nothing or completely telegraphed; they're all obnoxious Jump Scares punctuated with the same heavily clipped, unimaginative audio. Worse still, the game is outright horribly optimized — despite being done with the Unity engine, it suffers regular framerate drops.
