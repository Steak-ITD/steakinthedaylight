What is a Dream but a Shattered Memory (Friday Night Funkin)

This was based on a very odd dream I had. I was practically describing the events again, from the perspective of Girlfriend. It's the first time I tried writing a story in second-person POV, and it was actually quite interesting trying to describe stagnation and sensory deprivation as you go through a repetitive experience. I got pretty far into this one before other stuff took my interest. it was going to end with her finding a mangled, distorted Boyfriend in a room filled with blue light. He would reach out to her and she would be frozen in fear, which she isn't used to, then wake up confused and tired.

You feel like you've been walking forever. it probably hasn't been more than an hour. but time was always weird in places like this.

 

This hallway went on and on. There was nothing to see but walls with doors. All empty, all locked. There was no noise, other than an air filter from nowhere, and your own footsteps. The lights were a warm, dirty yellow, matching the pale yellow of the walls, and complimenting the red and green speckled carpet below.

 

That was it. Nothing to do but walk, since all the doors were locked. It just made you sad, the lack of any kind of activity. You thought about humming to get rid of the dullness, but it didn't help. The echo only made you feel more lonely. So you just keep walking forward, occasionally looking side to side. Usually, the monotony would frustrate you. But this time, it only makes you feel empty. It even gives you a sense of "dread". ...Just push it back into your mind.

 

By the time something changes, you feel numb.

 

The lights above begin to flicker, making a sound to match. As numb as your senses were, that was enough to get you to stop walking and look up. They flickered as if the earth was shaking, but you didn't feel anything. Once it stops, you look back in front of you. To your surprise, something else is different. One light up ahead stayed off. Not a very big change, but by this point, any change in the scenery was welcomed. You walk over to the light, a bit faster than usual.

 

Underneath the broken bulb, you notice another light. Blue, instead of the rotting yellow. Your eyes follow it to see that it's coming from the door to your left. The light glowed from underneath the door, and through its small glass window. You grab the knob and turn it. It moves. This door was actually unlocked! You feel excited. A different light, a different feeling from everything else around here. Maybe something fun was behind this door! New sounds, maybe a new song? The more you imagined, the more excited you felt. You open the door.

 

The blue light is much brighter inside. It surrounds the entire room but it doesn't have a source. The walls are a bit ornate, having a pattern in between and towards the ceiling. In the middle of the room is a plain-looking bed. Something dark covered the white sheets. You think it might be red, but it's hard to tell with the blue light. You look to the middle of the bed to find the source of the color.

 

Dread fills you again.

He really should just go home. It wasn't that far from here. That's what Pico tried to tell himself, as he stood in front of the window of Beta's apartment. How fortunate for him that it was on the first floor.

 

He had been out all day doing work, in this cold dreary, rainy weather. He was stressed, and he was freezing, and that wasn't good for a reptile. But, here was a warm place, a warm body... he didn't even know if Beta was home. He did forget to turn his lamp off often.

 

He had been on Pico's mind a lot lately. Weirdly so, to him. Lately, he seemed to fixate on all these small aspects of him, his voice, his smile, his scent. They were stuck in his head. It made him feel... hungry, in a sense. That wasn't too strange to feel about his boyfriend, wasn't it?

The Joltik would be on a journey to defeat it, only to find Corvisquire is actually a nice guy. Eventually the rest of the town tries to kill Corvi and he eats Joltik temporarily to scare them off.   • The Corvisquire lives up on a hill. He is introverted and greets Joltik skittishly.
  • Joltik is confused about how shy the bird seems to be and wants to talk.
  • Corvi lets him in and explains he's not very outgoing like the rest of his kind. He prefers to stay in and write.
  • Joltik is curious about his writing and Corvi explains they are adventure stories based on the places he used to travel.
  • joltik enjoys stories like these, and the two bond over their shared interest and write together.
  • Corvi gains the confidence to go outside again and explore more places and create more stories.
  • The spider community sees Corvi flying and assume he is out hunting, believing their village will be next.
  • They decide to sacrifice one of their own to get the bird to leave them alone.
  • Joltik volunteers, secretly to prove Corvi isn't dangerous.
  • They get to the hill. Corvi comes out and freezes in panic at all the electric spiders.
  • Joltik whispers to Corvi to show them he isn't dangerous, but Corvi suddenly eats him.
  • Corvi declares the "sacrifice" to be "worthy" enough, trying to sound bold and struggling to hide his stutter.
  • When the spiders leave to celebrate, Corvi spits Joltik out.
  • Joltik is upset about what Corvi did. It terrified him. Corvi explains he panicked and just wanted the spiders to go away, and apologizes profusely.
  • joltik is still upset because he can't go home after everyone thinks he's dead, lest they harass Corvi. Corvi offers for him to stay, but Joltik doesn't want to after what happened.
  • Corvi apologizes again. now Joltik is just sad he broke his trust. he leaves for somewhere unknown.
